## EVIDENCE WEIGHTING METHODOLOGY

### How Publication Tiers Work

**Tier 1 (20-25 points):** Major mainstream media with editorial standards
- Examples: ESPN, NY Times, BBC, Fox Sports, Reuters
- Why they matter: USCIS gives highest weight to established national/international outlets
- Documentation: Full article screenshots, publication metrics, editorial standards

**Tier 2 (15-20 points):** Industry trade publications and official sources
- Examples: UFC.com, Sherdog, MMA Fighting, industry magazines
- Why they matter: Field-specific expertise and credibility
- Documentation: Industry authority evidence, readership data, specialization proof

**Tier 3 (10-15 points):** Regional media and specialized outlets
- Examples: Bloody Elbow, international outlets, regional newspapers
- Why they matter: Shows broader recognition beyond Tier 1/2
- Documentation: Publication background, audience size, editorial process

**Tier 4 (5-10 points):** Minor outlets, Wikipedia, local sources
- Examples: Wikipedia, local papers, blogs, social media
- Why they matter: Supporting evidence, shows breadth
- Special considerations: Wikipedia needs notability analysis

## APPENDIX: PUBLICATION ASSESSMENT FRAMEWORK

### How USCIS Evaluates Publications

**From Matter of Chawathe (1989):**
- "Major media" must have significant circulation/reach
- Editorial oversight distinguishes from user-generated content
- National or international scope preferred
- Specialized trade journals acceptable if recognized in field

**From Policy Manual:**
- "Major media" includes newspapers, periodicals, professional publications
- Must be about the beneficiary, not just mentioning them
- Material must relate to beneficiary's achievements in the field

**Best Practices:**
- Lead with Tier 1/2 sources in petitions
- Use Tier 3/4 as supporting evidence only
- Calculate and present total reach metrics
- Show geographic diversity
- Demonstrate sustained coverage over time
